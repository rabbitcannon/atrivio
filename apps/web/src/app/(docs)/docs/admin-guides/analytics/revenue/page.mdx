# Revenue Analytics

Analyze your revenue streams with detailed breakdowns by attraction and ticket type.

## Overview

The **Revenue Analytics** page provides in-depth financial analysis:

- Gross and net revenue tracking
- Refund and discount monitoring
- Revenue distribution by attraction
- Revenue distribution by ticket type
- Detailed performance tables

![Revenue Analytics Dashboard](/docs/revenue-analytics.png)

## Accessing Revenue Analytics

1. Log in to your account
2. Select your organization
3. Click **Analytics** in the sidebar
4. Select the **Revenue** tab

**Note:** Revenue Analytics requires a **Pro** or **Enterprise** subscription.

## Filters

### Time Period

Select the date range for your analysis:

| Period | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Today | Current day only |
| Yesterday | Previous day |
| Last 7 Days | Rolling week (default) |
| Last 30 Days | Rolling month |
| Last 90 Days | Rolling quarter |
| Last 12 Months | Rolling year |

### Attraction Filter

Filter data by specific attraction:
- **All Attractions** - Combined data across all venues
- **[Specific Attraction]** - Data for one attraction only

## Summary Cards

Four financial metrics displayed at the top:

### Gross Revenue

Total revenue from all sales before any deductions:
- Sum of all ticket purchases
- Includes service fees charged to customers
- Does not subtract refunds or discounts applied

### Net Revenue

Actual revenue after deductions:
- Gross revenue minus refunds
- Minus discounts from promo codes
- This is your true earnings figure

### Refunds

Total amount refunded to customers:
- Full refunds
- Partial refunds
- Chargebacks and disputes

### Discounts

Total discount value applied:
- Promo code discounts
- Automatic discounts
- Percentage and fixed-amount discounts

## Revenue Over Time Chart

A line chart showing daily revenue trends:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **X-axis** | Date within selected period |
| **Y-axis** | Revenue amount in dollars |
| **Line** | Daily gross revenue |
| **Hover** | Exact revenue for each day |

**Insights:**
- Identify peak revenue days
- Spot seasonal patterns
- Track growth over time
- Plan for high-demand periods

## Distribution Charts

### Revenue by Attraction

A pie chart showing how revenue is distributed across your attractions:

- Each slice represents one attraction
- Percentage shows share of total revenue
- Hover for exact revenue amounts
- Color-coded legend for easy reference

**Use cases:**
- Identify your highest-earning attraction
- Balance marketing efforts
- Make investment decisions

### Revenue by Ticket Type

A pie chart showing revenue distribution by ticket category:

- Standard vs. premium ticket performance
- VIP and special experience revenue
- General admission vs. specialty tickets
- Time-slot based revenue (if applicable)

**Use cases:**
- Optimize pricing strategy
- Understand customer preferences
- Plan inventory and availability

## Performance Tables

| Column | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Attraction** | Name of the attraction |

| Column | Description |

## Revenue Analysis Tips

### Calculating Average Order Value

```
Average Order Value = Gross Revenue / Total Orders
```

A higher AOV indicates customers are buying more per transaction.

### Identifying Revenue Drivers

1. **Top Attractions**: Focus marketing on high-performers
2. **Premium Tickets**: Track if upsells are working
3. **Time Trends**: Identify your most profitable days

### Monitoring Refunds

High refund rates may indicate:
- Customer satisfaction issues
- Pricing concerns
- Weather-related cancellations
- Process or product problems

### Discount Effectiveness

Track discount usage to understand:
- Promo code effectiveness
- Customer price sensitivity
- Marketing campaign success

## Common Questions

### Why is net revenue lower than gross?

Net revenue subtracts:
- Refunds issued to customers
- Discounts applied via promo codes
- Any other deductions

### How often does data update?

Revenue data updates in real-time as:
- New orders are placed
- Refunds are processed
- Promo codes are applied

### Can I export revenue data?

For detailed financial exports:
1. Access your Stripe Dashboard for payment data
2. Use the Orders page for order-level exports
3. Export PDF from the Overview tab for reports
