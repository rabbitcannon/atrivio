# Attendance Analytics

Track guest check-ins and identify attendance patterns across your attractions.

## Overview

The **Attendance Analytics** page helps you understand guest behavior:

- Total check-in counts
- Check-in rates (actual vs. expected)
- Peak attendance times
- Attendance distribution by attraction

![Attendance Analytics Dashboard](/docs/attendance-analytics.png)

## Accessing Attendance Analytics

1. Log in to your account
2. Select your organization
3. Click **Analytics** in the sidebar
4. Select the **Attendance** tab

**Note:** Attendance Analytics requires a **Pro** or **Enterprise** subscription.

## Filters

### Time Period

Select the date range for your analysis:

| Period | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Today | Current day only |
| Yesterday | Previous day |
| Last 7 Days | Rolling week (default) |
| Last 30 Days | Rolling month |
| Last 90 Days | Rolling quarter |
| Last 12 Months | Rolling year |

### Attraction Filter

Filter data by specific attraction:
- **All Attractions** - Combined data across all venues
- **[Specific Attraction]** - Data for one attraction only

## Summary Cards

Four attendance metrics displayed at the top:

### Total Check-Ins

The actual number of tickets that have been scanned and checked in:
- Counts each ticket scanned
- Includes re-entries if allowed
- Real-time count as guests arrive

### Tickets Sold

Total tickets purchased during the selected period:
- Represents expected attendance
- Compare with check-ins to measure show rate
- Includes all ticket types

### Check-In Rate

Percentage of sold tickets that were actually used:

```
Check-In Rate = (Total Check-Ins / Tickets Sold) Ã— 100
```

**Interpreting the rate:**
- **90-100%**: Excellent show rate
- **70-89%**: Good, typical range
- **50-69%**: Moderate, some no-shows
- **Below 50%**: High no-show rate, investigate

### Peak Attendance

The highest number of check-ins recorded in a single time period:
- Shows your busiest moment
- Includes the date and time
- Useful for capacity planning

## Check-Ins Over Time Chart

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **X-axis** | Date within selected period |

## Attendance by Attraction

- **Percentage** - Share of total attendance
- **Progress bar** - Visual representation

**Use cases:**
- Identify most popular attractions
- Balance crowd flow between venues

## Understanding Attendance Patterns

### High Check-In Rate (Above 90%)

Indicates:
- Strong customer commitment
- Effective reminders working
- Good weather/conditions
- High-demand events

### Moderate Check-In Rate (70-89%)

This is typical and indicates:
- Normal no-show rate
- Room for improvement
- Consider reminder systems

### Low Check-In Rate (Below 70%)

May indicate:
- Recent sales not yet attended (future dates)
- Weather cancellations
- Customer satisfaction issues
- Pricing/value concerns

## Attendance Planning Tips

### Staffing Optimization

1. **Review peak times**: Staff heavily during peak attendance
2. **Day-of-week patterns**: Adjust weekend vs. weekday staffing
3. **Seasonal trends**: Plan for high-traffic seasons

### Capacity Management

1. **Track utilization**: Check-ins vs. capacity shows utilization
2. **Identify constraints**: Find bottlenecks at high-traffic attractions
3. **Balance flow**: Use data to spread guests across attractions

### Reducing No-Shows

1. **Send reminders**: Email/SMS before visit date
2. **Easy rescheduling**: Allow date changes to reduce cancellations
3. **Flexible policies**: Consider weather-related flexibility
4. **Incentives**: Offer perks for confirmed attendance

## Common Questions

### Why are check-ins higher than tickets sold?

This can happen when:
- Re-entry is allowed and counted
- Multiple check-ins per ticket
- Group tickets scanned individually

### Why is my check-in rate low?

Possible reasons:
- Tickets purchased for future dates
- Weather-related no-shows
- Customer cancellations
- Technical issues with scanning

### How do I improve attendance rates?

Strategies:
- Send reminder emails/SMS
- Offer flexible rescheduling
- Create urgency with limited availability
- Improve the overall guest experience

### Can I see hourly attendance?

Peak attendance shows the busiest time. For detailed hourly data:
- Use the Check-In Reports page
- Filter by specific date ranges
- Export for detailed analysis
