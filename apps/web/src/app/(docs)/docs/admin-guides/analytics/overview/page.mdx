# Analytics Overview

Get a comprehensive view of your organization's performance with the Analytics dashboard.

## Overview

The **Analytics Overview** page provides a high-level summary of your key business metrics:

- Total revenue across all attractions
- Order counts and trends
- Ticket sales volume
- Check-in performance

![Analytics Overview Dashboard](/docs/analytics-overview.png)

## Accessing Analytics

1. Log in to your account
2. Select your organization
3. Click **Analytics** in the sidebar

**Note:** Analytics requires a **Pro** or **Enterprise** subscription.

## Filters

### Time Period

Select the date range for your analysis:

| Period | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Today | Current day only |
| Yesterday | Previous day |
| Last 7 Days | Rolling week (default) |
| Last 30 Days | Rolling month |
| Last 90 Days | Rolling quarter |
| Last 12 Months | Rolling year |

### Attraction Filter

Filter data by specific attraction:
- **All Attractions** - Combined data across all venues
- **[Specific Attraction]** - Data for one attraction only

### Refresh

Click **Refresh** to reload the latest data.

## Summary Cards

Four key metrics displayed at the top:

### Total Revenue

Gross revenue from all ticket sales during the selected period. This includes:
- Ticket purchases
- Add-ons and upgrades
- Before refunds and discounts

### Orders

Total number of completed orders. Each order may contain multiple tickets.

### Tickets Sold

Total quantity of individual tickets sold across all ticket types and attractions.

### Check-Ins

Number of tickets that have been scanned and checked in at your attractions.

## Charts

### Revenue Over Time

A line chart showing daily revenue trends:
- **X-axis**: Date
- **Y-axis**: Revenue amount
- **Hover**: View exact revenue for each day
- **Trends**: Identify peak sales days and patterns

### Orders Over Time

A bar chart displaying daily order counts:
- Useful for identifying busy periods
- Compare with revenue to calculate average order value
- Plan staffing and inventory based on trends

### Attendance Over Time

A bar chart showing daily check-in counts:
- Track actual guest attendance
- Compare with tickets sold to measure show rates
- Identify peak attendance days

## Export Options

### Export to PDF

Click **Export PDF** to generate a downloadable report containing:
- All summary metrics
- Charts and visualizations
- Current filter selections
- Generation timestamp

**Use cases:**
- Share with stakeholders
- Keep for records
- Include in presentations

## Understanding Your Metrics

### Revenue Health

- **Consistent growth**: Revenue trending upward indicates healthy operations
- **Spikes**: Identify what drove high-revenue days (events, promotions)
- **Dips**: Investigate low periods (weather, competition, seasonality)

### Order Patterns

- **Order volume**: Track customer demand
- **Average order value**: Revenue / Orders gives you AOV
- **Peak times**: Plan capacity and staffing

### Attendance Metrics

- **Show rate**: Check-ins / Tickets Sold shows attendance rate
- **No-shows**: Tickets sold but not checked in
- **Walk-ups**: May indicate demand for on-site sales

## Tips for Better Analytics

1. **Regular Review**: Check analytics weekly to spot trends early
2. **Compare Periods**: Use different date ranges to compare performance
3. **Attraction Analysis**: Filter by attraction to identify top performers
4. **Export Reports**: Keep PDF exports for quarterly/annual reviews
